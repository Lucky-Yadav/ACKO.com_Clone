What is bike insurance/two-wheeler insurance policy?

Bike insurance or a two wheeler insurance policy is a contract between the bike owner and the insurance provider. Here the insurance company provides financial coverage for damage/loss to the bike owner in exchange for a premium

Types of bike insurance policies on ACKO

Our bike insurance plans cover different types of two wheelers such as motorcycles, mopeds, scooters, and more.

Third-party Bike Insurance Plan

Third party Bike Insurance is mandatory in India. It protects you against financial liability if you accidentally damage someone else's vehicle or property or injure someone in an accident. However, it doesn't offer financial protection against damages or losses to your bike.

Comprehensive Bike Insurance Plan

A Comprehensive Bike Insurance covers everything that Third-party insurance covers, and also covers losses or damages to your two-wheeler in case of an accident, theft, fire, natural calamities (floods, earthquakes), riots, or strikes.

Personal Accident (PA) Cover

It is an additional cover to enhance your bike insurance plan. It is mandatory by law to own a PA Cover in India. While your 2 wheeler insurance policy covers damages to your bike, the PA Cover provides financial protection in the event of disability/death while riding a two-wheeler

Exclusions: What is not covered in two-wheeler insurance by ACKO?

While we aim to give you optimum coverage, ACKO's Comprehensive bike insurance policy has the following exclusions.

Breakdown caused due to electrical or mechanical issue.

Accidental damages under the influence of alcohol/intoxicating drugs.

Accidental damages during war or similar conditions.

Any claims against an incident when your insurance policy was not active

Cost of repair/replacement of parts that are damaged due to regular wear and tear and not related to any incident.

Claims related to tyre or tube punctures (You get 50% of repair/replacement cost if your bike is damaged at the same time).

Any claims if you were driving without a valid licence or do not own a vehicle or using a vehicle for an illegal purpose

Claims related to damages caused outside the geographical boundaries of the country.
